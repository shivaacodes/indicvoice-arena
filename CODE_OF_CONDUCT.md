# Contributor Covenant Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We as members, contributors, and leaders pledge to make participation in our community a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, caste, color, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

We pledge to act and interact in ways that contribute to an open, welcoming, diverse, inclusive, and healthy community.

---

## Our Standards

Examples of behavior that contributes to a positive environment include:

- Demonstrating empathy and kindness toward others  
- Being respectful of differing opinions, viewpoints, and experiences  
- Giving and gracefully accepting constructive feedback  
- Taking responsibility for mistakes and learning from them  
- Focusing on what is best for the community as a whole  

Examples of unacceptable behavior include:

- Sexualized language, imagery, or unwelcome sexual attention  
- Trolling, insulting, or derogatory comments, and personal or political attacks  
- Harassment in public or private  
- Publishing others' private information without explicit permission  
- Any conduct that could reasonably be considered inappropriate in a professional setting  

---

## Enforcement Responsibilities

Community leaders are responsible for clarifying and enforcing standards of acceptable behavior. They may take appropriate corrective action in response to unacceptable behavior.

Community leaders may remove, edit, or reject contributions that violate this Code of Conduct and will communicate moderation decisions when appropriate.

---

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to all community spaces, including GitHub repositories, discussions, issues, pull requests, and official social media channels.

It also applies when representing the community in public spaces, such as using official email addresses, posting via official accounts, or participating in events as a project representative.

## Enforcement Guidelines

Community leaders will follow these guidelines when responding to violations:

### 1. Correction

**Community Impact:** Minor inappropriate behavior or unprofessional language.

**Consequence:** A private written warning explaining the violation. A public apology may be requested.

---

### 2. Warning

**Community Impact:** Repeated or serious violations.

**Consequence:** A formal warning and temporary restrictions on interaction. Further violations may result in a ban.

---

### 3. Temporary Ban

**Community Impact:** Sustained inappropriate behavior or serious violations.

**Consequence:** Temporary ban from community participation. Violating ban terms may lead to a permanent ban.

---

### 4. Permanent Ban

**Community Impact:** Pattern of harassment, abuse, or discrimination.

**Consequence:** Permanent removal from all community spaces.
